How Orlando's Floridan Aquifer Water Affects Pool Surfaces

Central Florida's water chemistry is shaped by the Floridan Aquifer System ‚Äî one of the most productive aquifer systems in the world, underlying most of Florida and parts of Georgia and South Carolina. While geologically similar to Miami's Biscayne Aquifer, the Floridan Aquifer typically produces softer water in the Orlando area.

Floridan Aquifer: Soft, Corrosive Water

Orlando's municipal water measures approximately 120 mg/L calcium carbonate ‚Äî classified as "Moderately Hard" but chemically undersaturated relative to pool plaster's calcium content. This means the water will actively pull calcium carbonate from your pool surface to reach equilibrium.

The result over months and years:

  • Etched surfaces ‚Äî rough, sandpaper-like texture underfoot
  • Gray mottling ‚Äî uneven calcium depletion creates patchy appearance
  • Accelerated plaster thinning ‚Äî surface literally dissolves over time
  • Shortened lifespan ‚Äî plaster lasts 7‚Äì10 years vs. 10‚Äì15 nationally

Management: Maintain calcium hardness at 250‚Äì350 ppm (above the 200‚Äì400 standard range's midpoint), test bi-weekly, and dose with calcium chloride regularly. This won't eliminate the problem in plaster pools ‚Äî it only slows it.

Why Central Florida Defaults to Pebble Aggregate

It's no coincidence that pebble aggregate finishes (Pebble Tec, StoneScapes, Mini Pebble) dominate the Orlando market. Local contractors learned decades ago that white plaster simply doesn't last in Central Florida's soft-water environment.

Pebble aggregate surfaces contain rounded quartz, pebble, and mineral particles that are:

  • Far less soluble than calcium carbonate plaster
  • Resistant to the etching and pitting process
  • Able to last 15‚Äì20 years with proper care in Orlando water
  • More attractive, with natural color that holds up under Central Florida UV

The $2,000‚Äì$4,000 premium over plaster typically pays for itself in avoided early resurfacing costs over a 15-year window ‚Äî especially at Orlando's current contractor pricing.
