Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know when my pool needs resurfacing?

Look for rough or sharp surfaces that scratch swimmers, visible cracks, peeling plaster, persistent staining that won't brush off, or water loss beyond normal evaporation. Most pools need resurfacing every 7‚Äì15 years depending on the surface type and local water chemistry.

How long does pool resurfacing take?

The process typically takes 5‚Äì10 days: 1‚Äì2 days to drain and prep, 1‚Äì2 days for the new surface application, and 3‚Äì7 days for curing before you can swim. Weather, surface type, and pool size affect the timeline.

Can I resurface my pool myself?

Pool resurfacing is not a DIY-friendly project. It requires specialized equipment, professional-grade materials, and in most states (TX, AZ, FL, NV) a contractor's license. Improper installation voids material warranties and can lead to premature failure.

What's the difference between resurfacing and replastering?

"Replastering" specifically refers to applying new white plaster. "Resurfacing" is the broader term covering all surface types ‚Äî plaster, pebble, quartz, fiberglass. All involve draining the pool, removing the old surface, and applying a new one.
