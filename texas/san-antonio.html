How San Antonio's Water Affects Your Pool Surface

üíß

Edwards Aquifer: Very Hard Water

San Antonio's drinking water comes from the Edwards Aquifer, a limestone karst formation that produces water at approximately 340 mg/L calcium carbonate ‚Äî classified as "Very Hard." For comparison, the national average is about 100 mg/L.

This mineral-rich water accelerates calcium carbonate scale buildup on pool surfaces, particularly on textured finishes like pebble and quartz. Pool owners should:

  • Maintain calcium hardness between 200‚Äì400 ppm
  • Use a calcium sequestrant product regularly
  • Schedule acid washing every 3‚Äì5 years
  • Consider fiberglass coatings for maximum scale resistance

Surface Life Impact

Unmanaged hard water can reduce pool surface lifespan by 20‚Äì30%. A plaster surface that typically lasts 10‚Äì15 years nationally may only last 8‚Äì12 years in San Antonio without proper chemistry management.

Our recommendation for San Antonio: Pebble Tec or quartz aggregate finishes. They're more resistant to calcium scale than bare plaster, and their textured appearance hides minor deposits between maintenance cycles.

Frequently Asked Questions ‚Äî San Antonio Pool Resurfacing

How much does pool resurfacing cost in San Antonio?

Pool resurfacing in San Antonio typically costs $4,000‚Äì$8,500 depending on surface type. Basic white plaster runs $3,200‚Äì$6,000, pebble aggregate (Pebble Tec) costs $5,500‚Äì$10,500, and quartz finishes range $4,800‚Äì$9,000. These prices assume a standard 450 sq ft residential pool.

Does San Antonio's hard water damage pool surfaces?

Yes. San Antonio's Edwards Aquifer water measures approximately 340 mg/L calcium carbonate ‚Äî classified as "Very Hard." This accelerates calcium scale buildup on pool surfaces. Regular acid washing every 3‚Äì5 years and using a calcium sequestrant helps manage this. Unmanaged hard water can reduce surface life by 20‚Äì30%.

Do pool contractors need a license in Texas?

Yes. Texas requires a Texas Pool and Spa Contractor License (TPCL) from the TDLR. The license requires passing an exam and posting a surety bond. Always verify your contractor's license at tdlr.texas.gov.

What's the best pool surface for San Antonio?

Given SA's 134 days above 90¬∞F and very hard water, pebble aggregate or quartz finishes are recommended over basic plaster. They're more resistant to calcium scale and UV degradation. Fiberglass coatings offer the best scale resistance but are less common for full resurfacing.

When is the best time to resurface a pool in San Antonio?

October through February is ideal. Pool usage is lowest, contractors have more availability (often better pricing), and cooler temperatures provide more predictable cure times for plaster and pebble surfaces. Avoid resurfacing in July‚ÄìAugust when extreme heat can cause rapid curing issues.

The Pool Resurfacing Process: What to Expect

Understanding the resurfacing process helps you plan your project and evaluate contractor proposals. Whether you're in San Antonio or anywhere in the United States, the core steps are consistent ‚Äî though the timeline varies based on surface type and pool condition.

1Ô∏è‚É£

Inspection & Assessment (Day 1)

A licensed contractor inspects the existing surface, checking for structural cracks, delamination, calcium deposits, and plumbing integrity. They'll measure the pool's square footage and test the water chemistry to identify any pre-existing imbalances. This assessment determines the scope of prep work required ‚Äî a major cost variable.

2Ô∏è‚É£

Drain & Surface Prep (Day 1‚Äì2)

The pool is drained using submersible pumps (typically 4‚Äì8 hours for a standard pool). The old surface is chipped or blasted away using hydroblasting, acid washing, or mechanical chipping, depending on surface type and condition. Cracks are patched, the shell is inspected again, and the surface is roughened for the new finish to bond properly.

3Ô∏è‚É£

Surface Application (Day 2‚Äì3)

New plaster, pebble, or quartz is applied by hand in multiple layers. Plaster is typically one smooth coat; pebble/quartz is a two-coat process ‚Äî a bonding coat followed by the aggregate finish. Work typically takes 6‚Äì12 hours for a standard pool. The surface must be applied quickly and evenly to prevent visible seams.

4Ô∏è‚É£

Fill & Start-Up Chemistry (Day 3‚Äì7)

The pool is refilled (18‚Äì36 hours for a standard pool) and the contractor begins the "start-up" chemical regimen. This critical phase involves daily brushing to remove plaster dust, precise chemical balancing, and monitoring for the first 30 days. Improper start-up chemistry is the #1 cause of premature surface failure ‚Äî especially in markets with hard or soft water. Your contractor should provide a written start-up protocol.
